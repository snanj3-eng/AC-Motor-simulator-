Core principle in one sentence

No slip -> no induced rotor EMF -> no rotor current -> no electromagnetic torque.

Step-by-step physical story

  1. 3-phase stator currents create a rotating magnetic field at synchronous speed (Ns = 120f/P).
  2. Because rotor is slower, the field sweeps past rotor conductors (relative speed = slip speed).
  3. This relative motion induces rotor EMF and current (fr = s·f).
  4. Rotor current creates its own magnetic field, interacting with stator field to produce torque.
  5. The rotor accelerates, reducing slip, until electromagnetic torque balances load torque.

Practical experiments to try

  • Experiment A: Keep poles fixed, increase frequency. Notice both field speed and rotor speed increase.
  • Experiment B: Keep frequency fixed, increase poles. Synchronous speed drops.
  • Experiment C: Move slip close to zero. Orange bars fade and torque estimate drops.
